## üîß Usage Guidelines

### For `/news-update` Command:
1. **Priority scan:** Foundation models, design tools (MCP), creative AI
2. **Secondary scan:** Industry/VC news, creator platforms
3. **Tertiary scan:** Tool aggregators for emerging tools
4. **Focus on:** Last 7 days of updates

### For General Research:
- Use Wired, TechCrunch, The Information for context on major developments
- Check a16z and YC for market perspective
- Verify tool launches via Product Hunt or official blogs
- Cross-reference multiple sources for accuracy

### What to Track:
‚úÖ **Do track:**
- Major model releases (GPT-5, Claude 4, Gemini 2, etc.)
- New design tool features (especially MCP integrations)
- Creative AI breakthroughs (video, image, audio)
- Dev days and keynote announcements
- Funding rounds for AI startups
- Platform algorithm changes
- Tool launches relevant to content creators

‚ùå **Don't track:**
- Consumer tech unrelated to AI/design
- Political AI regulation debates (unless directly impacts tools)
- Academic papers without practical applications
- Crypto/web3 (unless AI intersection)
- Hardware announcements (unless AI chips/tools)

### Source Reliability Tiers:

**Tier 1 (Most Reliable):**
- Official company blogs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, etc.)
- Wired, The Information, Stratechery
- a16z, Y Combinator

**Tier 2 (Generally Reliable):**
- TechCrunch, The Verge, Protocol
- Product Hunt, Future Tools
- Creator platform official blogs

**Tier 3 (Verify Before Using):**
- AI tool aggregator sites
- Newsletter summaries (use as leads, verify with Tier 1/2)
- Social media rumors (always verify)
